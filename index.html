Middle School

I received my first computer, a Toshiba laptop, in elementary school. Aside from playing around with Scratch, my mom signed me up for some coding practice on Tynker and that is when I first realized coding is fun! I would spend days leading up to my family members' birthdays making them games on Tynker. It was something original that I could create and share with them, and I loved it! This led way to middle school where we had so-called talents that we had to try out for to get into the school. I tried out for the computer-math talent and got in! This was when I took my first real coding classes. In 6th grade we mostly played around with SketchUp, a 3D modeling program, and flight simulators. In 7th grade, it was all about robotics and we used Arduino. Then in 8th grade, that's when HTML, CSS, Python, and Photoshop came into play. Even though part of the school year was online due to the COVID pandemic, I learned so much about web development that year and really enjoyed it.

High School

During my time at Stuyvesant, I spent time during both the school year and the summer working on computer science. During freshman year, I took an online javascript course from Johns Hopkins University about interactive web development. I learned so much about HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that year and it really solidified my interest in web development. Then during the next school year (my sophomore year), I took Annual Intro to CS, which went into Dr. Racket, Netlogo, and Python. During the summer after freshman year I joined the KWK community when I participated in the iOS Mobile Application Development summer camp. This was truely such a unique experience and only solified my appreciation for software development by introducing me to the world of app development. I took AP Computer Science A during my junior year, where I learned Java and object-oriented programming. I also took part in a Google Mentorship program where I worked in a group of 3 other students and one mentor from Google on a project, ours being a website that primarily relied on React. After junior year, I participated in a three-week program at Columbia Univeristy where I learned about data science using Python and machine learning principles. Unfortunately, during my senior year, the programming office didn't place me in any computer science classes, but I was really happy with how much I learned during my time at Stuyvesant.

College

At Cornell Engineering, I came in as a prospective computer science major. After countless college tours, I settled with Cornell University as my top choice specifically because of its strong computer science program and unique classes. During my first semester I took CS 1998 - Introduction to iOS Mobile Development, a student-led class that taught us both UI Kit and SwiftUI, delving into frontend development, backend integration, and Figma design integration. Second semester I took CS 2110 - Object-Oriented Programming and Data Structures, which utilized Java as the primary language. During my freshman year, I also took on a campus job as a student web developer for a professor within the Linguistics department, where I worked on a website for his research utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I also joined the technical computer of WICC (Women in Computing at Cornell), where I worked on a project to create a website for the organization using React and aided in creating and conducting a Git presentation for the club. After completing my first year, I was already able to apply for affiliation with the computer science major and got accepted! During the summer after my freshman year, I moved across the country to Seattle, Washington for an internship at Amazon as a software development engineer intern on the alerting mobile team. I would have never found my love for mobile development if it weren't for Kode with Klossy, and I am so grateful for that!
